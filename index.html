Our Mission


The Provincetown Public Art Foundation is dedicated to telling the story of Provincetown's, Past, Present and Future with Public Art.



Public Art Projects


The Breath of the Moon

“ The Breath of the Moon: A Tidal Ecology of Provincetown Harbor Project” by Mark Admas to be sited on the waterfront seawall of Provincetown. Commissioned by the Provincetown Public Art Foundation

Mary Heaton Vorse

“Mary Heaton Vorse Memorial” (model for full scale sculpture, in progress) by Penelope Jencks to be sited at 466 Commercial St. (the Vorse House) Provincetown, MA. Commissioned by the Provincetown Public Art Foundation

Foundation Fund

"Time and the Town” mural detail by Esteban Del Valle sited at 245 Commercial St, Provincetown, MA. Commissioned by the Provincetown Public Art Foundation

Marine Salvage Right Whale Sculpture

Cape Artist Wendy Klemperer is working with the Center for Coastal Studies to create a life size Right Whale from welded marine salvage, dredged from off of Provincetown. The PPAF is working with the Center for Coastal Studies and NOA to develop this project for the town.

Wind Driven Sculpture

Wind Artist Andrew Mowbray has created a wind-driven drawing sculpture that autonomously creates graphics from the wind. It will be installed at the new Bryant Saltworks temporary art site. The site is dedicated to George Bryant’s love of wind power.

The Provincetown Public Art Foundation was founded in January, 2020, by Romolo Del Deo, Lise King, Karen Ojala, Stan Sikorski & Samuel Tager. Our mission is to tell the story of Provincetown's, Past, Present and Future with Public Art. We are a Federally recognized 501(c)3 Non-Profit Organization incorporated in Provincetown, MA.
